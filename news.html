Featured Article: It’s Okay To Not Be Okay, leads Netflix PH’s Top 10 Shows and premiere week ad loads​​

A new story unfolds on ABS-CBN’s Primetime Bida Block when It’s Okay To Not Be Okay premiered on their on-air platforms (Kapamilya Channel, A2Z, Jeepney TV, and TV5, and online via Kapamilya Online Live) last July 21, 2025 (new same day episodes are released on weekdays at 20h45, after FPJ’s Batang Quiapo). The series was led by Anne Curtis, Joshua Garcia, and Carlo Aquino as it takes viewers on an emotional journey of pain, love, and healing in the Philippine adaptation of the critically acclaimed Korean drama that premiered not only through on-air, but also on Netflix 72 hours prior to airing and iWant 48 hours beforehand. 

It's stellar cast are Rio Locsin, Bobot Mortiz, Michael De Mesa, Maricel Laxa, Agot Isidro, Enchong Dee, Kaori Oinuma, Bodjie Pascua, Ana Abad Santos, Sharmaine Suarez, Xyriel Manabat, Louise Abuel, Francis Magundayao, Alora Sasam, Alyssa Muhlach, Bianca De Vera, Aljon Mendoza, and Mark Oblea, with Mae Cruz-Alviar and Raymond Ocampo were the director of this series, and it is produced under Star Creatives.

In an ABS-CBN Press Release involving the series posted last July 5, 2025, it follows an emotionally guarded psychiatric ward caregiver Patpat (Joshua) who has dedicated his life to caring for his older brother Matmat (Carlo), a person with autism.

Their world changes when they cross paths with a successful yet troubled children’s book author Mia (Anne), who is known for her cold and eccentric personality. As Mia, Patpat, Matmat confront painful childhood traumas and emotional scars, they begin a journey of healing, self-acceptance, and love.

Reimagined through a uniquely Filipino lens, the series promises a deeply emotional narrative that explores trauma, healing, and the power of love in the face of psychological struggles.

When the series was premiered on Netflix (releasing its episodes 72 hours before airing), it took over in the first spot among all of the TV Shows on Netflix Philippines’ Top 10 TV Shows listing, while it also ranked second among all of the top 10 shows on the other online streaming platform from where the series is available, iWant (releasing them 48 hours in advance), as of July 25, 2025.

Then the July 21, 2025 on-air premiere came in to Kapamilya Channel, A2Z, TV5, and Jeepney TV, when we, DRPS PHCA, made a TV Ad Listings between It’s Okay To Not Be Okay, against its competitor, Sanggang Dikit, For Real, on GMA (under same timeslot basis). Based on our TV Ad Listings between the two shows, the series led by Anne, Joshua, and Carlo had aired a total of 70 TV Ads among all of the three commercial gaps on Kapamilya Channel, with 64 of them are from A2Z simulcast, and 65 on TV5 simulcast and Jeepney TV delayed airing. This is comparable with Sanggang Dikit For Real’s 10 TV Ads on GMA – which is seven times higher based on our same timeslot final count of 22-24-24 (Kapamilya Channel) vs. 4-3-3 (GMA).

Turning to the peak concurrent viewers (via ABS-CBN Entertainment YouTube Channel) and it’s TV Ratings (via Nielsen NUTAM Preliminary/Overnight Report, also same timeslot), It’s Okay To Not Be Okay was seen by about 608,595 of Kapamilyas online, and it’s been voted by about 7.5% of viewers (c.
